Why Budapest?

Budapest, queen of of the Danube

What is Budapest like? This beautiful and exciting historical city is is filled with culture, tradition and vital energy. Contrasts such as great and small, ancient history and recent times, untroubled quiet spots and the upbeat locations all come together to make up what we know as Budapest.

The Parliament building facing the River Danube is the symbol of the city, while the Castle of Buda on the opposite side is a symbol of history and condition of our identity. It is also a World Heritage site. The surrounding castle district offers the most romantic walks in Budapest. Some of the sights you can find there include the Holy Trinity Square, the Matthias Church and the Fishermen's Bastion.

Restaurants and bistros

Great places to eat out in Budapest

Veranda

Veranda is a restaurant and pizzeria serving mediterranean dishes, and it offers vegetarian and vegan options as well.

Address:

Kopaszi gát 3, Budapest 1117

What I like about it

The restaurant is on Kopaszi gát, a piece of land reaching into the river Danube. On sunny days, sitting outside surrounded by nature while having a good pizza is an experience of its own.

Ropogó

Fried and crunchy food - both meta and vegan options available - with really creative salads or other dishes on the side.

Address:

Múzeum körút 41, Budapest 1053

What I like about it

There's a huge variety of dishes to choose from and also set menus for mode indecisive folks like me who'd like to try something new.

Komachi

A Japanese bistro located in the center of Budapest, and yet it's still cosy to sit in. It serves various Japanese dishes and also has sets if you're unsure of what to try.

Address:

Kertész u. 33, Budapest 1073

What I like about it

I especially recommend the soup with miso base and their curry.
